World Happiness

the happiness level of different nations based on the ratings of people’s lives in that country

The origin of World Happiness can be ascribed to the Bhutanese Gross National Happiness index. Back in 1972, Bhutan started evaluating happiness rather than narrowing it to other simple factors such as GDP. This measure of collective happiness was eventually instituted officially by the government of Bhutan on 18 July 2008. In 2011, United Nation General Assembly was inspired by the use of this great notion and passed Resolution “Happiness: towards a holistic approach to development”, which encourages the world to take Bhutan as an example and accelerates the widespread of the idea of world happiness.

A World happiness report is generated based on the data from the Gallup World Poll. The very basic foundation would be the Cantril Ladder survey, in which people are asked to rate their life from 0 to 10 (the worst being 0 and the best being 10). The poll will randomly select around 1000 people from each country (registered civilians of the country, aged 15 and older) and ask for the happiness survey in corresponding languages.
